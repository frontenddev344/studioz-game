Looking for a Shortcut? Here Are All the Vault Codes in Agent Zero: First Strike

Vaults are scattered throughout Agent Zero: First Strike, hiding valuable rewards, weapon upgrades, intelligence files, and rare resources. While the game encourages players to uncover each code through exploration and puzzle-solving, some operatives prefer to skip the detective work and get straight to the loot.

Why Vaults Matter

Every vault contains rewards that can give you a significant advantage during the campaign. From experimental gadgets and upgrade materials to hidden story documents, these locked rooms are often worth the effort required to access them.

How Vault Codes Are Found

Most vault codes are hidden within mission environments. Players may discover clues on computer terminals, security logs, surveillance footage, or notes left behind by enemy operatives. Some codes require combining multiple clues before the correct sequence becomes clear.

Should You Use Codes Right Away?

Using vault codes immediately can help speed up progression, but it may also reduce the satisfaction of solving environmental puzzles. If you're focused on collecting every reward or completing a second playthrough, using a code guide can save a considerable amount of time.

Benefits of Unlocking Every Vault

  • Gain access to rare weapons and equipment
  • Collect valuable upgrade components
  • Unlock hidden lore and intelligence reports
  • Earn additional credits and resources
  • Progress toward completionist achievements

Before You Enter Every Code

Some vaults contain mission-specific rewards that may be more useful later in the campaign. Consider your current loadout and upgrade path before claiming every item immediately. Returning to previously visited locations can sometimes provide a better strategic advantage.

Bottom line: Whether you're trying to collect every hidden reward or simply want a faster route to powerful gear, vaults remain one of the most rewarding side activities in Agent Zero: First Strike. Having the right codes on hand can turn a difficult mission into a much easier operation.
