Guap Preview – Survive a Deadly Casino in This Wild Co-op Roguelike

What happens when a gambling simulator collides with chaotic co-op survival gameplay? That's exactly the idea behind Guap, a fast-paced multiplayer roguelike where players must gamble their way out of an increasingly dangerous casino facility.

Instead of exploring abandoned planets or collecting scrap, you and your teammates are thrown into a high-risk environment where every spin, card draw, and dice roll could either save the group or ruin the entire run.

Designed around tension, teamwork, and unpredictable outcomes, Guap transforms casino games into survival mechanics. Each in-game day comes with a financial target your team must reach before time runs out.

One bad gamble can wipe out the entire team's progress, making every decision feel meaningful and dangerous.

A Co-op Casino Survival Experience

At its core, Guap is a multiplayer roguelike built for up to four players. The game focuses heavily on risk management, communication, and adapting to random events.

Unlike traditional casino simulators, Guap adds survival pressure to every decision. Players aren't simply chasing profits—they're trying to stay alive long enough to escape the facility.

The atmosphere combines neon-lit casino visuals with dark, unsettling environments that create a constant sense of tension.

Dynamic Gameplay and Daily Challenges

Every in-game day introduces randomized mechanics and game rotations. This keeps matches unpredictable and prevents players from relying on a single strategy.

Some rounds may focus heavily on card-based games, while others push players toward dangerous high-risk betting systems with larger payouts.

Players Must Carefully Decide:

  • When to cash out
  • Which teammate takes the risk
  • How much money to pool together
  • Whether to play safely or go all-in

Visual Style and Atmosphere

Guap leans heavily into a dark neon casino aesthetic mixed with psychological tension. Flashing lights, distorted audio, crowded tables, and sudden environmental changes create a stressful but exciting atmosphere.

The first-person presentation helps make every gamble feel personal, especially during high-stakes moments when teams are risking everything to survive another round.

Developer Information

Guap is being developed by indie studio BoMassy, with a strong focus on replayability, multiplayer chaos, and unpredictable roguelike systems.
