Best Point-and-Click Adventure Games to Play in 2026

Point-and-click adventure games continue to thrive in 2026 thanks to a mix of legendary classics, modern remasters, and creative indie releases. Whether you enjoy solving mysteries, exploring strange worlds, uncovering emotional stories, or tackling clever puzzles, the genre still offers some of the most memorable experiences in gaming.

From traditional inventory-based adventures to narrative-driven interactive stories, these are some of the best point-and-click adventure games worth playing right now.

From classic mysteries and emotional narratives to modern remasters and indie gems, these are the 10 best point-and-click adventure games to play in 2026.

10. Hero-U: Rogue to Redemption

Hero-U: Rogue to Redemption combines classic adventure-game puzzle solving with RPG progression systems. Instead of simply moving between puzzles, players also build character skills, manage daily activities, and shape how the story unfolds.

The game blends humor, exploration, and role-playing mechanics into a unique fantasy adventure experience. Its replay value and character customization help it stand out from more traditional adventure games.

Why Play It?

  • Mix of RPG and adventure gameplay
  • Character progression systems
  • Multiple story outcomes
  • Puzzle-solving and exploration
  • Fantasy academy setting

9. Night in the Woods

Night in the Woods focuses heavily on storytelling, atmosphere, and character development. Players follow Mae as she returns to her hometown and slowly uncovers emotional struggles and strange mysteries hidden beneath the surface.

While lighter on traditional puzzles, the game delivers one of the strongest narrative experiences in modern adventure gaming.

Why Play It?

  • Emotional storytelling
  • Memorable characters
  • Atmospheric world design
  • Strong dialogue and writing
  • Mystery-focused narrative

8. Loco Motive

Loco Motive brings classic comedy adventure energy into a modern murder mystery set aboard a luxury train. The game features eccentric characters, detailed pixel-art visuals, and clever puzzle design inspired by old-school point-and-click classics.

It perfectly captures the charm of retro adventure games while adding smoother modern gameplay systems.

Why Play It?

  • Murder mystery setting
  • Pixel-art presentation
  • Humorous writing
  • Traditional point-and-click gameplay
  • Modernized classic design

7. Life Is Strange

Life Is Strange leans more toward narrative adventure than traditional puzzle-heavy gameplay, but its strong emotional storytelling earns it a place on this list.

Why Play It?

  • Choice-based gameplay
  • Emotional narrative
  • Time-rewind mechanics
  • Cinematic presentation
  • Strong character development

6. Telltale's The Walking Dead

Telltale's The Walking Dead helped redefine narrative adventure games through emotional choices and survival storytelling.

Why Play It?

  • Emotional storytelling
  • Meaningful choices
  • Strong character writing
  • Accessible gameplay
  • Episodic narrative structure

5. Broken Age

Broken Age modernized classic adventure-game design with polished visuals, accessible puzzle systems, and dual protagonists.

Why Play It?

  • Beautiful hand-painted visuals
  • Beginner-friendly puzzles
  • Dual-story structure
  • Humorous writing
  • Modernized adventure mechanics

4. Machinarium

Machinarium remains one of the most visually distinctive point-and-click adventures ever created.

Why Play It?

  • Unique hand-drawn art style
  • Puzzle-focused gameplay
  • Minimal dialogue storytelling
  • Atmospheric environments
  • Creative puzzle design

3. Broken Sword: Shadow of the Templars – Reforged

Broken Sword remains one of the greatest mystery adventure games ever made. Players investigate conspiracies, solve historical puzzles, and travel across multiple countries while uncovering secrets connected to the Knights Templar.

Why Play It?

  • Excellent mystery storytelling
  • Historical conspiracy themes
  • Strong puzzle design
  • Memorable protagonists
  • Updated remastered visuals

2. Grim Fandango Remastered

Grim Fandango combines film noir storytelling with a surreal world inspired by the Land of the Dead.

Why Play It?

  • Unique world-building
  • Noir-inspired storytelling
  • Memorable characters
  • Stylish remastered visuals
  • Classic adventure-game charm

1. Return to Monkey Island

Return to Monkey Island successfully revives one of gaming's most iconic adventure franchises while remaining welcoming to new players.

Why Play It?

  • Excellent puzzle design
  • Funny dialogue and writing
  • Accessible for new players
  • Modernized interface systems
  • Strong nostalgic value
